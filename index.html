About

Originally from Irpinia and now based in Geneva, I am a Business Analyst with three years of international experience at Ralph Lauren, where I worked across EMEA and LATAM on data analysis, reporting, and performance management.

Educated in Economics and Management at Bocconi University (BSc and MSc), I have always been fascinated by how numbers tell stories — from monetary policy and business strategy to culture and current affairs.

I like to understand how systems work, but also how stories shape them: the way a country organises itself, how a film frames a moment, or how a glass of wine can tell where it comes from. For me, business analysis is another way of doing that — using data to make sense of reality, building clarity from complexity, and helping decisions become more aware and informed. It represents a form of structured curiosity, not so different from writing or watching a film with the wish to understand what lies underneath.

That curiosity also takes other forms. Through Borderliner, my personal newsletter, I explore the intersections of history, economics, and culture. On Letterboxd, I collect reflections on cinema — my favourite way to pause and look at the world. And as a certified sommelier, I continue to nurture my passion for wine and regional development, first explored in my master’s thesis on wine tourism in Irpinia.

Since moving to Switzerland, I have been continuing to grow both personally and professionally, with a deep commitment to understanding and integrating into its culture and society.

This website brings all of that together — the work I do, the things I love, and the curiosity that links them.
