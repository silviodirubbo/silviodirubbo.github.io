About

I am Silvio, Italian living in Geneva. I have always been drawn to how things connect — ideas, people, places, time. I like to understand how systems work, but also how stories shape them: the way a country organises itself, how a film frames a moment, or how a glass of wine can tell where it comes from.

I work in business analysis, which for me means using data to make sense of reality — building clarity from complexity, and helping decisions become more aware and informed. It is a kind of structured curiosity, not so different from writing or watching a film with the wish to understand what lies underneath.

Borderliner is where that curiosity takes another form. It is a personal project of essays and notes that connect history, economics, and culture. Cinema is my way to pause and look — I keep track of films and reflections on Letterboxd. And wine has been a passion since long before I knew much about it; now I try to learn one bottle at a time.

This website brings all of that together — the work I do, the things I love, and the curiosity that links them.
